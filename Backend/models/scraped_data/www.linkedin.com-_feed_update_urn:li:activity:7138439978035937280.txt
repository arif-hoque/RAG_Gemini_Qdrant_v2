Ramsay Brown my take 

The article on the ISDO Cycle is an exceptional contribution to the discourse surrounding the rapid advancements in AI tech, particularly focusing on Large Language Models (LLMs). It offers a comprehensive framework, aptly named ISDO (Innovate, Scale, Democratize, Optimize), which neatly encapsulates the complex, dynamic, and interrelated mechanisms driving the rapid evolution of LLMs
One of the strengths of this article lies in the depth of its analysis and the multidisciplinary insights it incorporates. The Innovate phase, for instance doesn't just talk about machine learning as an isolated field but borrows from neuroscience and cognitive sciences to provide a well-rounded perspective. This multidisciplinary approach provides a more nuanced understanding of the tech progress we are witnessing
This doesn't shy away from discussing the real-time changes affecting the ISDO Cycle. The Scale phase, which is often considered to be a straight process of adding more computational power, is shown to have its caveats. Recent developments indicate that the old adage "Scale is all you need" is evolving. This temporal aspect makes the article not just a snapshot but more of a dynamic update on the state of LLMs
The discussion around the Democratize phase is particularly relevant. The article delves into how open-source contributions and dissemination via platforms like arXiv, Github, and Twitter are essentially crowd-sourcing the cycle's efficiency. This is not just an observation but an acknowledgment of a decentralized network's power, which broadens the base of contributors and quickens the pace of innovation
The Optimize phase, with its focus on parameter quantization and pruning, speaks volumes about the technological feasibility of these advanced models. It's not just a theoretical debate; the article connects the dots between the lab and real-world applications, explaining how these optimized models can be deployed in edge devices and how they are becoming more environmentally friendly
This also acknowledges the diverse group of stakeholders, from Frontier Model labs like Google and OpenAI to venture capital firms like A16Z. It showcases the ecosystem as a collaborative and symbiotic space, rather than a competitive arena, which is refreshing and closer to the reality of scientific and technological advancements
The blog does not ignore the elephant in the roomâ€”governance. By pointing out the challenges in AI governance, it makes a compelling case for why understanding the ISDO Cycle is crucial not just for technologists but also for policymakers
The blog serves as both a lens to view the current landscape and a compass to navigate the future of LLMs. It combines rigorous analysis with a wide-ranging scope, making it an indispensable read for anyone interested in the future of AI and machine learning. It doesn't just tell us where we are; it gives us a comprehensive toolset to understand why we are here and where we are headed
